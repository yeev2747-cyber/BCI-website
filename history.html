Timeline

The development of Brain-Computer Interfaces has progressed from early discoveries of brain activity to modern implantable technologies capable of restoring communication and controlling digital devices.

1924

First Human EEG Recording

German psychiatrist Hans Berger successfully recorded the first human EEG signals. His invention of the electroencephalograph laid the scientific foundation for modern Brain-Computer Interface research.

1977

First Laboratory BCI Experiment

Researchers demonstrated one of the earliest Brain-Computer Interface systems, using EEG signals to move a computer cursor. This showed that brain activity could be translated into computer commands.

1988

Brain-Controlled Robot

Stevo Bozinovski and colleagues successfully controlled a robot using EEG signals. This milestone demonstrated that brain activity could be used to operate a physical device in real time.

2011

Wireless EEG Headsets

EMOTIV introduced one of the first commercially available wireless EEG headsets, making Brain-Computer Interface technology more accessible for research, education, and consumer applications.

2017

Brain-Controlled Formula One Car

Rodrigo Hübner Mendes became the first quadriplegic person to drive a Formula One car using only brain signals captured by an EEG headset, demonstrating the real-world potential of BCI technology.

2024

Neuralink Human Demonstration

Neuralink demonstrated an implanted Brain-Computer Interface that enabled a participant to control a computer cursor and play video games using only brain signals, highlighting recent advances in implantable BCI systems.
