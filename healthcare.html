How Brain Computer Interface act as an Assistive Technology

Graphical Representation of BCI Usage of Different Types of Conditions

Did you know?

Around 50 million people worldwide have epilepsy, making it one of the most common neurological diseases globally.

Source: World Health Organization

Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCIs) help people with neurological disorders overcome everyday challenges and improve their quality of life.

The following table explains how BCIs assist patients with various neurological conditions:

Table 1: Common patient challenges, BCI solutions, and their benefits.
Challenge Faced by Patients BCI Solution / Mechanism Outcome / Benefit
Loss of motor function (stroke, spinal cord injury) Detects motor intentions from brain signals and controls robotic exoskeletons, prosthetic limbs, or virtual rehabilitation tools. Improved limb mobility, faster recovery, and enhanced independence.
Inability to communicate (ALS, locked-in syndrome, severe stroke) Decodes neural signals for speech or intent, enabling typing, speech generation, or communication device control. Restored communication, improved social interaction, and reduced emotional isolation.
Cognitive deficits, attention problems, depression, anxiety, and PTSD Uses neurofeedback and BCI-assisted cognitive therapy to monitor and regulate brain activity. Improved focus, emotional control, and cognitive rehabilitation.
Risk of seizures or disease progression (epilepsy, Parkinson's disease) Real-time EEG monitoring detects abnormal brain activity and predicts seizures. Timely interventions, reduced emergencies, and proactive disease management.
Dependence on caregivers for daily activities Brain-controlled wheelchairs, smart home devices, robotic assistants, and AI-powered adaptive control. Greater autonomy, improved quality of life, and reduced caregiver dependence.
Personalized rehabilitation and treatment Collects real-time neural data while AI analyzes patient-specific brain patterns to tailor therapy. More effective patient-specific interventions and accelerated research.

This table describes common patient challenges, the Brain-Computer Interface solutions used to address them, and the resulting healthcare benefits.
