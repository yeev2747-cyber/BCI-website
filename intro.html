What is Brain-Computer Interface

Explore how BCIs create a direct connection between the human brain and technology, allowing thoughts to be translated into actions without relying on physical movement.

A Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) is a technology that interprets your brain activity and translates it into actions. In simple terms, it allows a person to communicate, control, or interact with digital devices using only their thoughts.

They are assistive technology that offer hope to those suffering from neurodegenerative diseases and disorders such as:

  • Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)
  • Cerebral Palsy
  • Epilepsy
  • Parkinson's Disease
  • Multiple Sclerosis (MS)
  • Locked-in Syndrome
  • Spinal Cord Injury
  • Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)
  • Paraplegia
  • Quadriplegia (Tetraplegia)
  • Stroke

Think about turning on a lamp. Normally, your brain decides what you want to do, your muscles carry out the movement, and then the lamp responds. A BCI removes that middle step.

How BCIs Work

Most BCI architecture follow these same general steps:

Signal Acquisition

The first stage of a BCI system involves recording brain activity using methods such as EEG. Electrodes placed on the scalp capture electrical signals generated by neurons. Different techniques vary in accuracy, cost, and invasiveness.
Other signal acquisition methods include:

  • ECoG, which records activity directly from the cortical surface
  • Intracortical microelectrodes implanted inside brain tissue
  • FMRI ,which measures blood-oxygen-level changes associated with neuronal activity.

Processing & Decoding

Raw brain data is filtered to remove noise (like eye blinks), allowing signal processing algorithms and machine learning to extract neural features (such as motor imagery via EEG) and translate them into commands within a closed feedback loop.

Output & Feedback

Once decoded, neural activity is converted into actionable outputs such as prosthetic movement or speech commands. Many BCIs use a closed-loop system where users receive real-time feedback.
